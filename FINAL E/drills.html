Closing Out & Drive with "Hips Across" Finish Drill

Objective:This drill focuses on improving defensive skills, closing out on offensive players, and executing a specific finishing move at the rim.

Setup:

  • - Two guards, one acting as the offensive player (with a basketball) and the other as the defender.
  • - The offensive player starts on the perimeter, and the defender is positioned in a defensive stance a few feet away.

Execution:

  • 1. The offensive player makes a move towards the basket, simulating an attempt to drive past the defender.
  • 2. The defender practices closing out quickly, maintaining a low defensive stance and attempting to prevent the offensive player from driving to the basket.
  • 3. If the offensive player successfully starts driving, the defender must execute a defensive slide and stay in front.
  • 4. The offensive player, upon reaching the vicinity of the rim, performs a move called "hips across." This involves using a lateral movement of the hips to create separation from the defender and finish the drive at the rim.
  • 5. Players switch roles after each repetition to practice both offensive and defensive skills.

Man-to-Man Defensive Rotation Drill

Objective:This drill is designed to enhance players' understanding of man-to-man defense principles and improve their ability to rotate and communicate effectively.

Setup:

  • - Players are divided into offensive and defensive teams, with each defensive player assigned to guard a specific offensive player.
  • - The offensive team is set up with players in various positions on the court, and the defensive team starts in a man-to-man defensive alignment.

Execution:

  • 1. The coach initiates ball movement among the offensive players, simulating a dynamic offensive play.
  • 2. Defensive players must communicate, switch, and rotate as needed to defend against the offensive actions.
  • 3. Emphasis is placed on maintaining proper defensive positioning, communicating switches, and closing out on shooters.
  • 4. The drill may involve specific scenarios, such as defending against screens or helping and recovering.
  • 5. Coaches provide feedback on defensive rotations, communication, and overall execution.
  • 6. Players rotate roles to ensure everyone has a chance to practice both offensive and defensive aspects of the drill.
